Notes on Daily Cash Activity Report, (DCAR):

The DCAR is basically the ‘till tape’ of the day’s transaction activity. As you can see from the sample, it is basically divided into two sections. The top portion lists all the sources of revenue and taxes retained, followed by balance sheet adjustments (items that will change your actual cash on hand) where deposits are recognized and applied, gift certificate sales noted, etc.

We are in the practice of making a few manual adjustments to reflect staff tips and purchases plus any ‘extraordinary’ items. (the sample shows $8.00 in staff tips, coded to ‘6025’) The total of the top sections of the sheet must balance with the lower section, that lists all the payments we have received, by type.

There is another series of related ‘appendices’ that provide full detail on things like deposits received and applied, items sold and things like that.

There are portions of this report that we are hoping to eliminate, however. For example, our present system requires ‘pre-payments’ to be separate but we’d like to see if we can simplify this by just including those as ‘deposits’, which they basically are, etc.

I won’t attempt to provide full detail here because this portion of the project will probably be amongst the most involved and we’ll have to do quite a bit of ‘back and forth’ to sort out things like recognizing deposits applied to check out bills and probably a few more head scratchers will present themselves.

How we set up the ‘transaction’ portion of the Transaction Screen will be challenging and there may have to be a combination of codes and dates looked at in order to feed this report. More detail later, suffice it to say that we probably won’t be able to key all of this sheet from something like ‘departure day = today’. (Please excuse me if it sounds like I’m trying to do your job, this may not be as big of a challenge to you as it appears to me.)

The sample will give you a good idea of what works for us now. We don’t mind the manual adjustments because at the end of the week, the daily reports are taken upstairs and entered into a matching spreadsheet manually. This gives a chance to really see what’s happening, catch errors, look for patterns and stay on top of our activity. If this report fed automatically into our Revenue Summary spreadsheets, errors could flow freely, we wouldn’t know about them for quite some time and they’d be difficult to trace.

We are open to any and all suggestions, but wanted you to have an idea of what works for us at the moment.

Report notes

  • These notes explain how the workflow currently uses the Daily Cash Activity Report (DCAR).
  • Important workflow note: the DCAR has two balancing sections — revenue/tax/adjustment activity at the top and payments received by type at the bottom.
  • Important workflow note: manual adjustments are expected and desirable, because staff use the daily reports as a checked manual handoff into weekly spreadsheet summaries.
  • Daily cash scope questions, including prepayment handling, are tracked in daily_cash_reporting.questions.
  • Important workflow note: the transaction portion of the Transaction Screen will need enough dates/codes/detail to feed the DCAR correctly.
  • Primary feature: daily_cash_reporting.feature.
