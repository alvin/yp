Scope overview

This scope brings together the operating principles, screen flows, reports, and daily cash workflow into one navigable reference for the lodge and implementation team.

Project principles: keep the interface simple, keep reports and folios close to current printed outputs, simplify how staff interact with guest and reservation screens, and preserve the accounting/reporting clarity used day to day.

Screen flow

Lookup Screen

Home screen for name, reservation number, date/date-range, and all-fields search; also opens new reservations, reports, and print flows.

Feature: lookup_screen.feature

Primary paths: Print Screen · Daily cash reporting

Guest details, guest notes, and past / in-house / future reservation history.

Feature: name_search_results.feature

Date-based reservation list with last name, first name, room, and transaction-screen selection.

Feature: date_search_results.feature

Transaction Screen

Main reservation screen for guest details, room moves, report notes, charges, deposits, payments, cancellation, re-booking, and single-record printing.

Batch and individual printing for daily reports and guest-facing forms.

Important operating notes

  • Reservations are stored only up to one year in advance.
  • Guest notes are office-facing and should not print to reports.
  • Reservations may involve multiple guest names, one-bill arrangements, room moves, and split-bill needs.
  • Kitchen, housekeeping, and similar operational notes should stay close to the wording staff already use.
  • The DCAR remains a major scope area: it must balance, support manual adjustments, and stay close to current practice.
  • Unresolved flow alignment items are tracked in the features/*.questions files beside the related feature files.
