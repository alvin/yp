> I balance the day by comparing revenue and taxes against receipts, then trace variances to appendices.

You review and balance the Daily Cash Activity Report and appendix reports as part of the lodge’s daily closing routine. You need traceability from totals to deposits, payments, and cashed-out items.

## Goals

- Review revenue/tax/adjustment totals for the selected day
- Confirm payments-by-type totals and the balance owed
- Trace totals to deposits received/applied, cashier detail, and cashed-out items
- Ensure manual handwritten adjustments remain visible for balance reconciliation

## Frustrations

- Totals that don’t match after manual adjustments because reconciliation isn’t clear
- Missing detail behind a DCAR total
- Unclear handling of US receipts vs Canadian conversion
- Appendices that don’t feel like part of the same workflow

## Balancing the till tape

You open daily cash reporting from the lookup screen for a chosen day, review the upper and lower balancing sections, and then use appendix reports to support any discrepancies. Manual adjustment practices remain part of the workflow so balancing stays consistent with the lodge’s habits.
