> I need dietary and allergy details to arrive clearly on the kitchen report.

You ensure the kitchen gets meal and dietary information in the language staff have always used. You rely on kitchen and meal reporting fed by guest-linked notes and on a filtered mode for the right date range.

## Goals

- Receive dietary restrictions and allergies for the day
- See guest-specific wording in multi-name reservations
- Use filtered kitchen report mode when needed
- Avoid missing kitchen notes because the wrong date range was printed

## Frustrations

- Kitchen reports that don’t include guest-specific wording
- Diet/allergy details being too generic or incomplete
- Filtered reporting not matching the selected date range
- Operational notes appearing on the wrong report type

## Kitchen note readiness

Before meal service, you rely on kitchen reporting to include dietary restrictions, allergies, and meal preferences. The wording needs to stay close to staff-entered language because kitchen teams use it directly.

## Filtered reporting for prep

When scheduling requires a narrower set of days, you use the filtered kitchen report mode so the same dietary and kitchen note data is shown compactly for the selected date or date range.
